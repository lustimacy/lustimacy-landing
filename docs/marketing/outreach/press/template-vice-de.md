3. **Both get actively rejected by ad platforms.** Meta, Google, TikTok reject both religiously-conservative marriage apps AND adult lifestyle apps. How do you launch when 95% of modern growth playbooks are closed to you? That's the core tension.

4. **Lustimacy launches in 2026 with a feature no other dating app has built: linked couple profiles.** Two accounts, one shared identity, one match stream. Sounds obvious; literally no incumbent has built it.

I'm happy to talk about any angle — what it's like building for communities the mainstream overlooks, what works, what doesn't, where the ethical lines are (privacy in lifestyle apps, cultural expectations in marriage apps). Founder-direct, no agency, no PR filter.
