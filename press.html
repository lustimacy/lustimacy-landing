About Lustimacy

Lustimacy is a mobile dating app built specifically for the European lifestyle community — couples (together or solo), open-minded singles, and adults exploring ethical non-monogamy. Founded by independent developer Abed Almoradi, it's launching first in Germany and the Netherlands in 2026, with a waitlist-based rollout limited to the first 1,000 founding members per country.

The product is structurally different from other dating apps in three ways:

  • Linked couple profiles. Two accounts link into one shared couple identity — matches and chats are unified.
  • Private photos that unlock at the mutual match. No paywall. No "please share your album" requests. Photos are encrypted at rest, metadata stripped, and become bilaterally visible the moment two users match.
  • Hand-verified profiles. Every founding member is verified manually (selfie matched to profile photo) by the team. Founding members get this free; for later cohorts it's a low one-time fee.

The launch markets are intentional: Germany and the Netherlands together host the most developed lifestyle community infrastructure in Europe — clubs, FKK venues, established events, decades of community history.

About the founder

Abed Almoradi is the solo founder of Lustimacy. He previously built Janerek, a marriage-oriented dating app for the Arabic-speaking diaspora, and continues to operate both apps independently from his base in Europe.

Abed built Lustimacy after spending years watching the lifestyle community use tools that were either built decades ago (Joyclub) or built for a different purpose (Tinder, Feeld). He's available for written or recorded interviews — see contact below.

Story angles

If you're considering coverage, here are angles we'd love to talk about:

  • Why building for niche, adult-content-adjacent markets is structurally hard in 2026. Ad platforms reject these markets, which kills the standard growth playbook — what does an alternative look like?
  • Solo founder building dating apps for two different communities. Abed runs both Janerek and Lustimacy in parallel. The technical and cultural challenges of doing both as one person.
  • The European lifestyle community in 2026. What's changed in the last decade, what's coming.
  • Linked couple profiles as a product idea. Most dating apps still treat couples as "two individuals with a tag." Why that's wrong and what changes when you fix it.
  • Private photos as a product baseline. Why "premium photo unlocks" creates the wrong incentives for trust-sensitive markets.
